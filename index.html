Step 3 — Vendor selection and routing
Once a member selects a service, the platform determines which vendor to redirect them to using the following logic.
1
Fetch vendor list by service and corporate
The platform calls an API returning all empanelled vendors for the selected service scoped to the member's corporate policy.
2
Single vendor — automatic redirect
If only one vendor is available, the member is redirected immediately. The redirect event is logged with member ID, service, vendor, and settlement mode.
3
Multiple vendors — filter by pin code and location
Member provides their pin code. Vendors are filtered by geography. If one remains, automatic redirect. If multiple remain, member selects. Note: location logic is currently hardcoded for TCS and must be generalised for all corporates.
4
Phase 2 — Prioritisation engine
A backend rule engine returns a ranked vendor. Supports manual priority, round-robin rotation, and randomisation. Member selection is no longer needed — the result is consumed transparently by the interface.
Cashless vendors
Medibuddy, Tata 1mg, Practo — integrated with cashless settlement. Member pays nothing at point of service.
Reimbursement vendors
ekincare, HealthifyMe, MFine — member pays at the counter and submits a reimbursement claim through the app.
Discount partners
Netmeds, Apollo 247, Cure.fit — no policy coverage. Member receives a volume-negotiated discount via coupon code.

Step 5 — Corporate configuration logic
Each corporate can have a different configuration. The platform must be entirely config-driven so adding, hiding, renaming, or overriding a service for any corporate requires no code change — only a configuration update via admin panel.
⚙
Service visibility — which services appear for a corporate
A corporate can suppress any service entirely
▼
Wellness suppressed: If a corporate runs its own internal wellness programme — a dedicated gym, in-house yoga sessions, or a contracted fitness vendor — they may choose to hide the Wellness service on MAven entirely so members are not confused by a parallel offering.
Dental and vision excluded: Some corporates do not offer dental or vision as part of their health benefit. These services are hidden from that corporate's members — not greyed out, simply absent from the catalogue.
All services visible (default): Unless explicitly configured otherwise, all available outpatient services appear for every member. The platform shows everything and labels each service by its settlement mode.
Configuration: corporate_service_config[service_id].visible = true / false
Managed via admin panel. Takes effect immediately without a code deployment.
✍
Service renaming — corporates can relabel service names
The display name shown to members is configurable per corporate
▼
Example — Wellness renamed to "Employee Wellbeing": A large corporate with brand guidelines may prefer their own terminology. The underlying service, vendor routing, and tracking all remain unchanged — only the label displayed to the member changes.
Example — Doctor Consultation renamed to "Doctor on Call": A corporate promoting a particular telemedicine product may want the service labelled as that product name for familiarity among employees.
Tracking rule: All internal logs, reports, and analytics must use the canonical platform service name — for example "Wellness" — not the corporate display name. Renamed labels are strictly for member-facing display and must never appear in data pipelines.
Configuration: corporate_service_config[service_id].display_name = "Custom Label"
Falls back to the platform default name if not set for the corporate.
💵
MediAssist discount services — when to show alongside or instead of policy benefits
The most nuanced configuration layer
▼
Corporate has no coverage for a service — always show MediAssist discount: If a corporate's policy does not cover pharmacy, the platform surfaces a MediAssist-negotiated discount — for example 15% off at Tata 1mg — as the primary offering. This is the Cohort D experience applied at the individual service level, not the entire member.
Corporate covers a service and opts in to showing MediAssist discount alongside: The member sees their policy benefit — cashless or reimbursement — as the primary option. A MediAssist discount appears as a secondary tile labelled "Additional savings available". The member can choose either path.
Corporate covers a service and opts out of MediAssist discount: Only the policy-entitled benefit is shown. No discount tile appears. This is common when a corporate has negotiated exclusive vendor rates separately and does not want MediAssist's volume discount to create confusion or conflict.
Corporate has its own empanelled vendor and does not want MediAssist vendors shown: The platform suppresses all MediAssist vendor options for that service and routes the member exclusively to the corporate's contracted vendor — for example, a corporate-contracted diagnostic chain like Metropolis or SRL.
Configuration keys:
corporate_service_config[service_id].ma_discount_enabled = true / false
corporate_service_config[service_id].show_ma_alongside_policy = true / false
corporate_service_config[service_id].ma_vendor_override = null | vendor_id
👥
Dependent member access — who in the family can use these services
Spouse and children on the same policy
▼
Dependants enabled (default for family floater policies): Spouse and up to two children can log in with their own credentials and access the same service catalogue as the primary member. Their cohort is determined by the same policy rules as the primary insured.
Dependants disabled: Some group health policies cover only the employee. The platform must suppress the service catalogue for dependant logins under such corporates — dependants should see a "not covered" state, not an error.
Service-level dependant restriction: A corporate may allow dependants to use pharmacy and laboratory tests but not wellness or doctor consultation. This is configured per service per corporate — not a single on/off toggle at the corporate level.
Configuration: corporate_service_config[service_id].dependant_access = true / false

Step 8 — Modular component architecture
The outpatient service component must not live only inside the OP section of the app. It must be built as an independent, self-contained module that can be invoked from any screen — claims, cashless approval, reimbursement, intimation, or any future touchpoint. If it is tightly coupled to a single screen, the pull strategy fails.
🏠
Claims submission screen
When a member submits a reimbursement claim for a lab test, the OP module appears inline: "Next time, book cashless directly through MAven." One tap opens the service with the member's pin code pre-loaded.
✅
Cashless pre-authorisation letter
When a cashless approval letter is sent — digitally or via email — a dynamic banner is embedded showing the outpatient discount available to this member. Configured per corporate, per service.
📋
Reimbursement approval letter
After a reimbursement is processed, the approval communication includes an outpatient nudge with the specific service category that was claimed — if a pharmacy claim was reimbursed, pharmacy cashless is promoted.
🏥
Hospitalisation intimation flow
When a member initiates a hospitalisation intimation, the module surfaces: "During recovery, you will need medicines and lab tests. Set up your outpatient services now." This is the single highest-intent moment in the entire member journey.
📱
Push notification and WhatsApp
The module can be triggered as a push notification or WhatsApp message with a deep link directly to the service catalogue. The discount percentage and pin code are pre-populated from the member's profile. No new screen needed.
🔒
Architecture requirement
The component exposes a single API call: resolve(member_id, service_hint?, location?). Any screen in the app or any communication channel calls this and receives the correct experience for that member's cohort. No duplication of logic.
Design principle: build once, invoke from anywhere. The outpatient module is not a page — it is a service. Any screen, letter, notification, or message can embed it with a single API call and a configurable discount value per corporate.
Step 9 — Per-corporate discount configuration
The discount percentage offered to members is not a platform-wide constant. Each corporate can have a different rate negotiated with MediAssist — Corporate A may offer employees 10%, Corporate B may offer 20%. This must be fully configurable without a code deployment.
💵
Corporate-level discount rate — configurable per service per corporate
Different corporates can show different discount percentages for the same service
▼
Scenario 1 — Standard discount: A mid-size corporate has negotiated a flat 10% discount for all outpatient services on behalf of their employees. Every member at that corporate sees "10% off" on every service tile in Cohort D or on MediAssist discount tiles in Cohort A, B, and C.
Scenario 2 — Premium corporate deal: A large corporate with high member volume has negotiated 20% on pharmacy and 25% on lab tests as part of an annual contract. Discounts are higher because the referral volume is higher. Different rates apply per service for the same corporate.
Scenario 3 — No MediAssist discount for this corporate: Some corporates have their own vendor contracts and do not want MediAssist's discount to appear alongside. The discount rate is set to zero or the discount tile is suppressed entirely for that corporate.
Banner and nudge content is discount-aware: When the OP module is invoked from a claim screen, approval letter, WhatsApp message, or push notification, the discount value shown to the member is pulled from this config in real time. The message "You can save 20% on lab tests" is dynamically generated — not hardcoded.
Configuration keys:
corporate_discount_config[corporate_id][service_id].discount_pct = 0–100
corporate_discount_config[corporate_id][service_id].discount_label = "20% off" | null
corporate_discount_config[corporate_id].discount_enabled = true / false
Step 10 — Pin code persistence and provider search
Two related requirements that change how members discover and access services. Once a pin code is given, it must be remembered. And beyond the redirect flow, members should be able to actively search for providers by service and location — like a health service finder within the app.
Pin code — remember, do not ask again
📍
Your current location is Mumbai — 400001. Do you want outpatient services in this area, or a different location?
Change
Once a member provides their pin code anywhere in the app, it is stored against their profile. On all subsequent outpatient service interactions, the saved location is used automatically — the member is shown a pre-filled confirmation prompt rather than a blank input field.
The confirmation prompt reads: "Your current pin code is [X]. Find services in this location or a different one?" This adds one tap instead of a full form entry — significantly reducing drop-off in the vendor selection step.
If the member is at a new location — for example, travelling for hospitalisation — they can update the pin code inline and the new location is used for that session only, without overwriting the saved home location.
API requirement: member_profile.last_known_pincode — read on every outpatient service screen load, written when member confirms or updates location.
Provider search — find services by location
Beyond the redirect flow, members should be able to search: "Is cashless lab test available near me?" — a discovery-first experience before they commit to a service.

Step 11 — Hospitalisation as the highest-priority trigger
Hospitalisation is not just one nudge point among many — it is the single highest-intent moment in the entire member journey. A hospitalised member will definitely need medicines, lab tests, and follow-up consultations. Failing to surface outpatient services at this moment means losing the member permanently as an outpatient platform user.
⚠ Hospitalisation is a 100% conversion trigger — treat it as priority one
During pre-authorisation: When a cashless hospitalisation pre-auth request is raised, surface the outpatient module inline — "During your stay and recovery, you will need medicines and tests. Set up your outpatient access now." The member is already in the app and highly engaged.
Discharge notification: When the discharge summary is generated or the hospitalisation is closed in the system, trigger a push notification: "You have been discharged. Your doctor likely prescribed medicines and follow-up tests — book cashless through MAven." Deep link to pharmacy and lab test services.
Within 48 hours of discharge: Send a WhatsApp message or email with the outpatient services module embedded — listing pharmacy, lab tests, and follow-up consultation options with the member's saved pin code pre-loaded. This is when the member is most likely to act.
Post-hospitalisation claim follow-up: When the inpatient claim is settled, include an outpatient nudge in the settlement communication — "Claim settled. Did you know you can book your follow-up tests cashless? No paperwork, no reimbursement needed."
The hospitalisation trigger requires the outpatient module to be wired into the inpatient claims and pre-auth workflows. This is only possible if the module is built as an independent component — which is why Step 8 (modular architecture) is a prerequisite for this trigger to work.
